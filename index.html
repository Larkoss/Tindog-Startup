We are HPF and our team consists of three medical students and two computer science students of University of Cyprus. Aiming to improve the daily lives of end-stage cancer patients and support the effort for a more personalized medical care, our team is designing two innovative tools.

First, the Reliefant application, which enables the patient to record daily the levels, the character, and the localization of his pain as well as the side effects that he or she may experience. In addition, they will be able to indicate when they are taking their analgesic medication (MST and PRN). All data will be linked to the doctors and nurses’ accounts to inform them regarding the effectiveness of his medication plan.

As for our second project, we are designing a device, opio-pop, in lollipop form, that will detect opiorphin levels in the patients’ saliva. Opiorphin is a new pain biomarker and will therefore contribute to its more objective perspective. Opiorphin levels will be send from the device to the application for health professionals to see.

The goal is a more personalized palliative care through telemedicine as well as an upgraded version of the existing way of performing the above operations. So, there will be a digital solution in the field of cancer pain. Finally, this will help overcome opiophobia as the patients’ relatives will understand the need for opioids through seeing the levels of opiorphin.
